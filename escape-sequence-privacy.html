Privacy Policy — Escape Sequence

// last updated: July 2026

Escape Sequence is a puzzle game made by Had Games. This policy explains what data the app handles.

What we collect

Nothing, directly. Escape Sequence has no accounts, no sign-in, and no servers of our own. Your progress, lives, and settings are stored only on your device. If you delete the app, that data is gone.

Advertising

The app shows ads through Google AdMob. To serve and measure ads, Google may collect device information such as your device's advertising identifier, IP address, and general usage data. On iOS, the app asks for tracking permission (the standard Apple prompt) before any of this happens:

— If you allow tracking, ads may be personalized using your advertising identifier.
— If you decline, you'll see non-personalized ads and no cross-app tracking identifier is used.

Either way, the game plays exactly the same. Google's handling of this data is described in Google's Privacy Policy and How Google uses information from sites or apps that use its services.

Purchases

In-app purchases (unlimited lives, remove ads, hints) are processed entirely by Apple's App Store. We never see your payment details.

Children

Escape Sequence is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this page with a new date.
